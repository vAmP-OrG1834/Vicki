Why the name Operation 1834?

When you saw our name, what did you think? If you guessed that 1834 has something to do with the year, you're correct! 1834 is the year that Canada abolished (got rid of, for those that don't know the meaning) slavery - or so we thought. To this very day, slavery is still a thing, believe it or not, since human trafficking is a form of modern-day slavery, whether it be forced labor, sex slavery, or other.

Okay, but what do we do?

In short, we are a Canadian organization that spreads awareness about human trafficking. Human trafficking is a serious issue that can affect anyone. Honestly, it's not talked about enough. Think about it. When was the last time a friend or family member actually mentioned human trafficking in a conversation and both actually started thinking about it? When was the last time it came up in school during a lesson? That's what we're here to do. To educate you and others about this horrific crime.

So what even is human trafficking?

Simple explanation: Human trafficking is the second largest and fastest-growing crime in the world. It's also one of the most misunderstood ones because it affects many different people and involves other crimes, and happens in many locations as well. There are six main kinds of trafficking:

    1. Labor trafficking
      • Harsh/dangerous working conditions
      • Paid less than minimum wage
      • Most victims are tricked into believing they are in debt
    2. Sex trafficking
      • Forced into acts of sex by deception, threats or violence
      • Happens in brothels, massage parlors, hotels, or online
      • Anyone under 18 is a victim
    3. Domestic Certitude
      • Forced to live in private homes, away from public view
      • Often verbally and/or physically abused
    4. Forced Marriage
      • Most commenly women and girls are coerced into marriage against their will, but men and boys can also be in a forced marriage
      • Usually the marriage is toxic
      • Sometimes tied to cultural or economic pressures
    5. Child trafficking
      • Children are trafficked for labor, sex, begging, or even adoption scams
      • Includes child soldiers, recruited and used in armed conflict, especially in war-torn regions.
    6. Organ trafficking
      • Victims are exploited for their organs, often under false pretenses or without consent
      • This is a lesser-known but deeply disturbing form of trafficking, especially in regions with poor healthcare access.

Why is it important to educate/be educated about human trafficking?

If you're a parent or teacher reading this, you probably want to know why you should educate your child(ren)/students. There's a few reasons why, but the main one is because if your child(ren)/students know as much as they can about human trafficking, it'll be more likely that they'll see the signs of human trafficking and know if they are or someone else is a victim.
